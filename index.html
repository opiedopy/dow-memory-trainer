Note: After February 29, 2800 there will be two calendars in use, the Revised Julian and the Gregorian. So, we stop at 2799. And before 1753, the USA and world used the Julian calendar, so we stop at 1753.

Jackie Dander came up with this method as the simplest memorization method for performing Day of Week tricks standing in front of audiences. It is just figuring out 6 numbers. He thinks you can master the memorization in a few days if you have talent for memorizing lists.

For Jackie, at age 78, he thinks it is a better brain exercise than crossword puzzles. He has honed his Day of Week skills to average 30 seconds. See if you can beat him! And smile when an audience member gives you an easy date, such as July 4, 2400. As you will find out, that date can be resolved in 3 seconds as Tuesday. Look at the app and there are many dates like that. Maybe quit during a presentation if you get an easy one like that. Bonus golly-whacker Hint: As you willl find out, the last step is to "modulo 7" but the super hint is that you can modulo-7 along the way anytime your addition gets to 7, 14, 21, 28, 35, etc, etc. That means if your date is December 26, hten N1 + N2 = 28 and that's zero after modulo 7 so note the zero and go on to N3-4-5-6 to finish calculations.
